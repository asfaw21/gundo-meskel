Dera Gundo Meskel

Explore the history, culture, and life of this unique Ethiopian town

Overview

Dera Gundo Meskel is a town located in the Oromia Region of Ethiopia. It serves as the administrative center of Dera Woreda, situated in the Kaba Shewa Zone. Geographically, it is positioned at approximately 10°13′24″N latitude and 38°42′1″E longitude, with an elevation of about 2,493 meters (8,179 feet) above sea level. The town is bordered to the south by the Jamma River, while the western, northern, and eastern boundaries are shared with the Amhara Region.

Demographics and Religion

The majority of the population practices Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity, with a significant Muslim minority. Gundo Meskel is the only urban settlement in Dera Woreda, with approximately 4% of the population living in urban areas.

Infrastructure and Services

  • Health center and private clinics
  • Preparatory and elementary schools, private kindergartens
  • Governmental offices and NGOs
  • Banking services and youth academy

Religious and Cultural Sites

  • Mekane Selam Meskele Eyesus Monastery: Established in 1426 by Emperor Zerayakob
  • Debre Yidra Kidus Giyorgis Church: Located on the eastern edge of Gundo Meskel
  • Debre Bisrat Kidus Gebriel Church: Situated on the western edge of the town

Natural and Cultural Highlights

The Abay (Blue Nile) river is one of the major natural landmarks near Dera Gundo Meskel.

The Jamma River borders the town to the south and provides scenic beauty and resources for the community.

Dera is known for its vibrant cultural activities, including traditional music, dance, and local festivals.
